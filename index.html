📖 Format Recommendation — Publish a Hardback Edition

The book is currently Kindle only. Every top-performing competitor in the beauty and lash space is a hardback — and there is a clear commercial reason for that.

✓
Price it correctly to make it worthwhile
KDP Print books have a minimum list price based on page count and trim size. The royalty is 60% of the list price minus the printing cost — so to clear at least $5 per copy, the book needs to be priced high enough to cover KDP's printing fee and still leave that margin on top. In a professional niche where buyers expect to pay for specialist content, a higher price point is entirely justified.
✓
Technique books are reference books — readers want them in print
Lash technicians and beauty professionals use guides on the job. A physical hardback sits on a shelf, gets flipped to mid-appointment, and gets recommended to colleagues. A Kindle edition doesn't serve that use case in the same way.
✓
Hardbacks perform better in Amazon Ads
Sponsored Products campaigns for physical books have higher click-through and conversion rates in the beauty category. The book cover is the ad — and a hardback cover carries far more visual weight in search results than a Kindle thumbnail.
✓
Having both formats doubles your listing reach
Once the hardback is live, both formats appear on the same Amazon listing page. Buyers choose their preferred format — and Nicola earns from both. The Kindle edition stays; the hardback adds a second, higher-revenue revenue stream alongside it.
