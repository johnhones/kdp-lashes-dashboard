Marketing Strategy
Step 1: Add Your Keywords to KDP

Adding the right keywords to your KDP listing is one of the highest-impact actions you can take right now. Amazon uses your keywords to decide which searches your book appears in. Without them, your book is invisible to buyers actively searching for lash training and technique guides.

✓
Use all 7 keyword fields
KDP gives you 7 keyword fields, each supporting up to 50 characters. Use every one. Think about how your reader searches, not how you would describe your book. Phrases like “lash technician guide” or “how to do eyelash extensions” are more valuable than broad single words.
✓
Target buyer intent, not genre
Do not repeat words already in your title or subtitle. Avoid misleading terms. Focus on what your buyer is actually searching for: professional lash training content, technique guides, and business-building for beauty professionals.
✓
Use the Readerbull keyword recommendations in this audit
The keywords section below contains research-backed terms for your niche, selected based on search volume, relevance, and competition level. Copy them directly into your KDP keyword fields.
↓
Step 2: Study Your Competing Books

Before promoting your book, understand what you are up against. The best-selling books in the adjacent beauty category are bestsellers for a reason. Study them and use what you learn to make your own listing, content, and positioning stronger.

✓
Study their covers, titles and descriptions
Look at the top 5 to 10 competing books. What do their covers have in common? How are their titles worded? What promises do their descriptions make? These choices are not accidental. Model what is working and identify where you can go further.
✓
Read their reviews carefully
Positive reviews tell you what buyers value most. Critical reviews tell you what is missing. If multiple readers say a competing book lacks advanced technique, that is a gap you can fill. Look for patterns across reviews, not individual opinions.
✓
Note the formats that sell
Every top performer in the beauty category is a hardback. Note their page counts, trim sizes, and price points. This data directly informs Step 4. Understanding why they sell in that format is the fastest way to position your own book to compete.
↓
Step 3: Build Ratings and Reviews

Amazon distinguishes between ratings and reviews. A rating is a star score with no written comment. A review includes written feedback alongside the stars. Both count towards your overall star rating and help your book appear credible to buyers, but written reviews carry more weight because they give shoppers context and confidence.

Building a mix of both is one of the most valuable things you can do before promoting.

✓
Ask for a rating or review inside your book
Add a short, genuine request at the back of your book asking readers to leave a rating or review if they found it useful. Add one in the middle too, as many readers never reach the final pages. Keep it natural and never ask for a 5-star review specifically.
✓
Use an ARC service to reach readers before your hardback launches
ARC services such as BookSirens and Hidden Gems connect authors with readers who have opted in to receive free books in exchange for honest reviews. These are legitimate, fully compliant with Amazon’s terms of service, and one of the most effective ways to build early social proof.
✓
Promote to lash and beauty professional communities
There are well-established communities on Facebook and Instagram for lash artists, beauty students, and salon professionals. Share useful content from the book rather than leading with a sales pitch. Demonstrating genuine expertise builds trust and makes readers far more likely to buy and leave a review.
↓
Step 4: Publish a Hardback Edition

Once you are confident in your listing and have some reviews, publishing a hardback edition is the move that unlocks the next level of revenue. Every top-performing competitor in the beauty and lash space is a hardback, and there is a clear commercial reason for that.

✓
Price it correctly to make it worthwhile
KDP Print books have a minimum list price based on page count and trim size. The royalty is 60% of the list price minus the printing cost. In a professional niche where buyers expect to pay for specialist content, a higher price point is entirely justified.
✓
Technique books are reference books. Professionals want them in print.
Lash technicians use guides on the job. A physical hardback sits on a shelf, gets opened mid-appointment, and gets recommended to colleagues. A Kindle edition does not serve that use case in the same way.
✓
Hardbacks perform better in Amazon Ads
Sponsored Products campaigns for physical books have higher click-through and conversion rates in the beauty category. The book cover is the ad. A hardback carries far more visual weight in search results than a Kindle thumbnail.
✓
Both formats appear on the same listing
Once the hardback is live, both formats appear on the same Amazon product page. Buyers choose their preferred format, and you earn from both.

↓
Step 6: Launch Amazon Ads

Once you have reviews and confidence in your listing, Amazon Sponsored Products advertising can become one of the most effective revenue channels available. The principle is straightforward: you invest a controlled daily budget, and if your campaign is optimised correctly, every pound you spend returns more than a pound in royalties.

There is real risk in getting it wrong, so seek support if you are unsure. Start with £5 to £10 per day, test the data, and only scale what is demonstrably working.

✓
Start with Sponsored Products on automatic targeting
When you first set up an ad campaign, choose the automatic targeting option. This lets Amazon decide which searches to show your book for based on its own data. Run it for two to four weeks on a budget of £5 to £10 per day before making any changes.
✓
Add manual campaigns targeting lash keywords after 4 weeks
Bid specifically on “how to do eyelashes”, “lash extension training”, and the lash business keywords identified in this audit. Pause keywords that spend without converting. Scale only what is working.
✓
Target 30 to 40% Advertising Cost of Sale
In a low-competition niche, this is very achievable and profitable. Monitor weekly and optimise bids against top-converting keywords. The Make-up Manual at £688/mo is a realistic 12-month benchmark once ads and reviews are in place.
